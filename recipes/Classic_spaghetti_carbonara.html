Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its creamy sauce made with eggs, cheese, pancetta (or bacon), and black pepper.

Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

Ingredient

  • 350g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 75g grated Parmesan cheese
  • 50g grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 52 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of salted boiling water according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. In a skillet, cook the diced pancetta or bacon over medium heat until crispy. If using garlic, add it to the skillet and cook until fragrant.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Parmesan, and Pecorino Romano cheeses. Season with black pepper.
  4. Once the spaghetti is cooked, drain it and return it to the pot. Immediately add the cooked pancetta or bacon and its rendered fat.
  5. SQuickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot spaghetti and toss everything together until the pasta is evenly coated and the sauce becomes creamy.
  6. Serve immediately, garnished with chopped parsley and additional black pepper if desired.
